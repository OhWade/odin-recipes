Southern Fried Chicken

Crispy Crunchy Southern Fried Chicken

This is the ultimate fried chicken recipe with a modern twist. This recipe is for true chicken loves! Some would even say it is finger licking good!

Ingredients

  • 1 whole chicken, cut into 8 pieces (legs, thighs, wings, breasts)
  • 2 cups buttermilk
  • 1 tablespoon hot sauce (optional, but adds a nice kick)
  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1⁄2 cup cornstarch (for extra crunch!)
  • 1 tablespoon salt
  • 1 tablespoon garlic powder
  • 1 tablespoon onion powder
  • 1 tablespoon smoked paprika (gives that smoky depth)
  • 1 teaspoon cayenne pepper (for heat, but adjust to your liking)
  • 1 teaspoon freshly cracked black pepper
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme (optional, adds herby flavor)
  • 4 cups Vegetable oil or peanut oil for frying (depending on your pan)

Steps

  1. Begin by cutting your whole chicken into 8 pieces. You can ask your butcher to do this for you, or if you’re feeling adventurous, grab a sharp knife and do it yourself (just follow the natural joints!). Now, place your chicken in a large bowl or ziplock bag. Add the buttermilk and hot sauce. Submerge the chicken in the liquid and let it marinate in the fridge for at least 2 hours, or overnight if you have the time. This step is key to keeping the chicken juicy and tender.
  2. In a shallow bowl or dish, combine the all-purpose flour, cornstarch, salt, garlic powder, onion powder, smoked paprika, cayenne pepper, black pepper, and dried thyme (if using). Whisk it all together until it’s evenly mixed. The cornstarch here is your secret weapon—it helps make the coating crisp and light, so don’t skip this step!
  3. After marinating, remove the chicken from the buttermilk. Let any excess liquid drip off, and then dredge each piece of chicken in the flour mixture. Make sure each piece is coated evenly and well. For extra crispy chicken, I recommend double-dipping: dip the chicken back in the buttermilk, then dredge again in the flour mixture. It adds an extra layer of crunch, and who doesn’t love extra crispy chicken?
  4. Pour the vegetable oil or peanut oil into a large, heavy-duty cast-iron skillet or Dutch oven, about 2-3 inches deep. Heat the oil over medium-high heat to 350°F (175°C). You can check the oil temperature by dropping in a pinch of flour—if it sizzles right away, you’re good to go. If you don’t have a thermometer, don’t worry; just use the “sizzle test.”
  5. Carefully add the chicken pieces to the hot oil, being sure not to overcrowd the pan. Overcrowding will lower the oil temperature, leading to greasy chicken. Fry in batches if you need to! Cook each piece for about 12-15 minutes on each side, turning them until golden brown and crispy. Use tongs to flip the chicken, and be gentle to keep that beautiful crust intact.
  6. The best way to check if the chicken is done is by using a meat thermometer. The internal temperature should be 165°F (74°C) in the thickest part of the chicken, especially in the thighs and breasts. If you don’t have a thermometer, the chicken should be golden brown on the outside and the juices should run clear.
  7. Once the chicken is cooked through, remove it from the oil and place it on a wire rack set over a baking sheet. This helps the chicken stay crispy and prevents it from becoming soggy. Let it rest for 5-10 minutes to allow the juices to redistribute inside the meat.
  8. Serve your crispy, crunchy fried chicken with some classic Southern sides like mashed potatoes, cornbread, or a fresh coleslaw. Trust me, it’s going to be a hit!
